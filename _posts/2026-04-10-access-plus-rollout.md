When we took Access+ to market, we had a choice: launch globally and move fast, or sequence regionally and learn first. We chose the latter — an APAC-first rollout that let us validate adoption patterns, surface integration edge cases, and build internal confidence before committing to a wider release.

This is the honest account of how that decision was made, how we structured the rollout, and what we learned about managing stakeholders who didn't initially agree with the approach.

## The challenge
{: id="the-challenge"}

Access+ was a meaningful product addition — not a feature, but a capability that changed how customers thought about their deployment. That meant it touched sales, support, regional operations, and engineering simultaneously. Each of those groups had different priorities, different risk tolerances, and different opinions about what "ready" looked like.
